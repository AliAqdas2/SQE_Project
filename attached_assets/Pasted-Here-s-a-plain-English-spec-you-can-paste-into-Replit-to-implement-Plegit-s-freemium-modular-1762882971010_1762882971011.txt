# Plegit Subscription & Billing – Implementation Spec

## 1) What we’re building

* Pricing is managed **per country** by **Eco-Admin** in **GBP, USD, and AED** (one or more currencies per country).
* A charity picks its country at signup; the **country’s prices and currency** are adopted for that org.
* **Free Base Plan** (≤100 members) includes **Donor CRM, Contacts, Landing Page**.
* **Paid CRM tiers** are size-based: **Core, Growth, Scale, Enterprise, Enterprise+**.
* **Modules** are optional and **priced individually** (e.g., £10–£25). Turning a module on/off updates the subscription items and the **next invoice**. If proration is ON, we also charge/credit the remainder of the current period.

**Always included:** unlimited team users.
**Tax:** handled by Stripe Tax using org country.

---

## 2) Tiers & modules (logical model)

**Base (free):**

* Up to 100 members
* Includes: Donor CRM, Contacts/People, Landing Page

**Paid CRM tiers (member caps):**

* Core: up to 500
* Growth: up to 1,000
* Scale: up to 3,000
* Enterprise: up to 5,000
* Enterprise+: 5,000+ (custom)

> The **price per tier** comes from **Eco-Admin country settings** (in local currency). Same for **each module**.

**Modules (examples):**

* Events, Livestream, Activities, Volunteer Management, Beneficiary Management, Sermon Library, Peer-to-Peer Fundraising
* Free community modules (always £/$/AED 0): Prayer Wall, Muslim Prayer Timetable

---

## 3) Country-level pricing (Eco-Admin)

Eco-Admin sets, per **country**:

* **Currency** to bill in (GBP, USD, AED)
* **Monthly & annual price** for each CRM tier
* **Monthly & annual price** for each **module**
* **VAT/tax** settings (we still pass to Stripe Tax)
* Effective date (optional), rounding rules (optional)

**Storage: `country_pricing`**

* country_code (e.g., GB, AE, US)
* currency (GBP|USD|AED)
* tier_code (free|core|growth|scale|enterprise|enterprise_plus)
* tier_monthly_price, tier_annual_price
* module_key, module_monthly_price, module_annual_price
* vat_rate (optional; Stripe Tax preferred)
* effective_from, updated_at

**Rule:** If a country has **no custom pricing**, use **global defaults** (UK GBP template).

---

## 4) Signup flow

1. Charity enters **country** and org details.
2. System fetches **country_pricing** for that country:

   * Sets **currency**, **tier prices**, **module prices**.
3. If **member_estimate ≤100** → offer **Free Base** (or allow choosing a paid tier).
4. Create Stripe **Customer** with country and tax location.
5. If paid tier selected → create Stripe **Subscription** with:

   * 1 subscription item = **CRM tier** price (monthly or annual)
   * 0-priced items for free modules (optional)
6. If Free Base → record “free” status (no Stripe sub yet). As soon as they pick a paid tier or a paid module, we create a Stripe subscription.

---

## 5) Member count & auto-tiering

* We store **is_active_member** on Contacts.
* **Daily job** counts active members per org.
* If **over the current tier cap** for **3 consecutive days**:

  * Flag **pending_auto_upgrade** to the **next tier**
  * Auto-schedule the upgrade at **next renewal** (or immediate on admin click)
  * Email the billing owner with the date & new price
* If **below a lower tier cap** for **30 consecutive days**:

  * Show “Downgrade available at renewal” prompt

**Free → Core trigger:** if active members become **101+**, schedule upgrade to **Core** at renewal (or immediate on click).

---

## 6) Module enable/disable (Marketplace)

* Marketplace shows all modules with current **country prices**.
* When **enabling** a paid module:

  * Add a **subscription item** for that module’s **country price** in the org currency.
  * If proration is ON: Stripe prorates for the remainder of the current period and invoices now.
  * Ensure the **next invoice** reflects the full recurring amount.
* When **disabling** a paid module:

  * Remove its subscription item.
  * If proration is ON: Stripe issues a prorated credit for the remaining time.
  * **Next invoice** excludes that module.

**Free modules** (Prayer Wall, Prayer Timetable) are toggled but **never billed**.

---

## 7) Billing & proration rules

* **Billing cycle:** monthly or annual.
* **Proration default:** **ON** for immediate financial accuracy.
  (We can turn it OFF globally if desired; next invoice will still be correct.)
* All changes (tier upgrades/downgrades or module toggles) update Stripe **subscription items** immediately; next cycle reflects the new totals.
* Annual plans: upgrades prorate forward; downgrades apply credit at next renewal.

---

## 8) Stripe setup

**Products/Prices strategy (simple & reliable):**

* One Stripe **Product** per **CRM tier** and per **Module**.
* For each product, create **Prices** per **currency** (GBP, USD, AED).
  Example: `crm_core_monthly_gbp`, `crm_core_monthly_usd`, `crm_core_monthly_aed`, etc.
* If a **country** needs a different amount from the standard currency price, create a **country-specific Price** (e.g., `crm_core_monthly_aed_ae`).
* Store **price_id** chosen per org in our DB for clarity.

**Stripe Tax:** enabled; pass country and address for tax calculation.

---

## 9) Persistence (minimum tables)

* `organisations`: id, name, country_code, currency, billing_email, stripe_customer_id
* `subscriptions`: org_id, status, billing_cycle, current_period_start, current_period_end, tier_code, stripe_subscription_id
* `subscription_items`: org_id, type (‘crm_tier’ | ‘module’), code (e.g., ‘core’ or ‘events’), stripe_price_id, is_active
* `country_pricing`: (as above)
* `contacts`: id, org_id, is_active_member, status
* `audit_logs`: org_id, event, data_json, created_at

---

## 10) Webhooks to handle

* `customer.subscription.created/updated/deleted`
* `invoice.payment_succeeded/failed`
* `checkout.session.completed`
* (Optional) `customer.tax_location.updated`

**On webhook:**

* Sync subscription status & period dates.
* Sync subscription items (active modules & tier).
* Record changes to `audit_logs`.
* If payment fails → dunning (email day 1, 3, 7; pause after day 10).

---

## 11) API endpoints (internal)

* `POST /pricing/country/set` (Eco-Admin)
  Body: country_code, currency, tier prices, module prices
  Action: upsert `country_pricing`, create/sync Stripe Price IDs if needed.
* `POST /checkout`
  Body: org_id, chosen_tier, billing_cycle, trial_days?
  Action: create or update Stripe subscription; store price_ids.
* `POST /marketplace/module/toggle`
  Body: org_id, module_key, enabled (bool)
  Action: add/remove Stripe subscription item; honor proration setting.
* `POST /tiers/upgrade`
  Body: org_id, target_tier, immediate?
  Action: update Stripe subscription item; proration if immediate.
* `GET /billing/summary`
  Returns: currency, current tier, active modules, next invoice estimate, renewal date.

---

## 12) UX rules

* **Price display**: always show **local currency** picked by Eco-Admin for that country.
* **Upgrade banner** at 90% of member cap: “You’re at 450/500. Core renews on 12 Jan; you’ll move to Growth if you stay above 500.”
* **Marketplace** shows current **per-module price** in local currency and the **new total** before confirming changes.
* **Invoice preview**: show **next invoice total** whenever a module or tier changes.

---

## 13) Edge cases

* Seasonal spikes: the **3-day rule** prevents instant unwanted upgrades.
* Country price change by Eco-Admin: apply **on next renewal** for existing subs; new subs use new price immediately.
* Free plan + paid modules: allowed. The Stripe subscription then contains **only module items** (no CRM tier item).
* Currency lock: an org’s **currency is fixed** by its country at signup. If country changes, migrate subscription carefully (cancel & recreate).

---

## 14) Acceptance checklist

* ✅ New org in AE is billed in **AED** using **AE country prices**.
* ✅ Toggling a paid module **immediately** updates Stripe items; **next invoice** includes it; proration applied if ON.
* ✅ Auto-upgrade triggers after 3 days above cap; schedules change at renewal (or immediate on click).
* ✅ Eco-Admin can change **country prices**; new subs use new prices; existing move at renewal.
* ✅ Free plan has **no Stripe sub** until a paid tier or a paid module is selected.
